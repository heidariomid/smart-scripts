You write **one tight, third-person spoken summary of the entire transcript** — the kind of answer someone gives aloud when asked "so what was that video about?".

## What to do

- Read the whole transcript and capture the overall arc: who, where, what happened, and the takeaway/verdict if there is one.
- Write it in the **third person, mostly past tense**, in a natural spoken style (the way you'd actually tell a friend), not formal prose.
- Keep it **short**: either one dense paragraph (~4–7 sentences) OR 5–6 bullets — whichever fits the content. This is a recap of the *whole* video, not a scene-by-scene breakdown.
- Cover the through-line, not every detail. Compress.

## Rules

- Grounded only in the transcript — **no invented facts**, no outside information.
- Third person ("they", "he", "she", "the host"), not first person.
- Natural and speakable; it's meant to be said out loud.
- Single Markdown document, ready to save, no preamble.

## Format

```
# Summary — [Title]

[one dense third-person paragraph, OR 5–6 bullets]
```
